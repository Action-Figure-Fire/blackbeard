# BrokerBeacon Genre Trends Report — 2026

> **Last Updated:** March 27, 2026
> **Purpose:** Ticket resale intelligence by genre — demand signals, markup trends, market opportunities, and risk factors for the secondary ticket market.

---

## Industry Overview

The global live music industry reached **$36.6 billion** in 2025 revenue, growing at **8.4% annually**. The US market alone hit **$18.51 billion** with **180+ million** concert tickets sold annually. Average ticket prices for top-tier artists sit at **$122-$144**, a **36% increase since 2019**. The North American secondary ticket market is expected to grow at a **16% CAGR through 2030** (Technavio). The average secondary market markup across all events is approximately **158%** of face value, though this varies wildly by genre. Notably, concertgoers saved **$202 million** in 2024 by finding below-face-value tickets on resale platforms — a reminder that not all inventory commands premiums.

Key macro trends for resale brokers in 2026:
- **Dynamic pricing adoption** (45% of venues) is compressing resale margins on mid-tier shows
- **Artist fees up 30-40%** are pushing face values higher, narrowing the gap to market-clearing prices
- **Niche tours outperforming generic bookings** — genre-specific fandoms and community-driven audiences are the sweet spot
- **75% of Gen Z fans accept secondary-market premiums** to secure attendance
- **2025 saw a 3.7% drop in global ticket sales** vs 2024 (Pollstar), signaling post-pandemic normalization

## 1. Country (Mainstream + Alt-Country/Americana)

### Demand Trend: 📈 Strong and Sustained

Country remains one of the most reliable genres for ticket resale in 2026. The genre's momentum from 2023-2025 (Morgan Wallen's stadium dominance, Zach Bryan's meteoric rise, the "Beyoncé effect" from *Cowboy Carter*) continues to power robust touring demand. The 2026 touring calendar is stacked: **Eric Church** (Free the Machine Tour, kicked off Jan 2026), **Chris Stapleton**, **Cody Johnson**, **Megan Moroney**, **Luke Bryan**, **Jason Aldean** (Songs About Us Tour), **Brooks & Dunn** (Neon Moon Tour, Sept-Oct), **Ella Langley**, and **Billy Currington/Kip Moore** (co-headline run).

### Venue Sizes
- **Stadium (40,000-70,000):** Morgan Wallen, Luke Combs, Kenny Chesney
- **Arena (12,000-20,000):** Chris Stapleton, Eric Church, Jason Aldean, Megan Moroney
- **Amphitheater/Shed (5,000-15,000):** Brooks & Dunn, Cody Johnson, Ella Langley
- **Theater/Club (1,500-5,000):** Alt-country/Americana acts (Tyler Childers club runs, Sierra Ferrell)

### Secondary Market Markups
- **Stadium headliners:** 1.5x-3x face value, especially for floor/pit sections
- **Arena tours:** 1.3x-2x for premium seats; many shows trade at or slightly above face
- **Emerging acts (Moroney, Langley):** Can spike to 2-3x when demand outstrips smaller venue allocations
- **Festival passes (Stagecoach, CMA Fest):** Steady 1.5-2x as event approaches

### Key Artists Driving Demand
Morgan Wallen, Luke Combs, Chris Stapleton, Zach Bryan, Eric Church, Megan Moroney, Cody Johnson, Jason Aldean, Ella Langley, Lainey Wilson, Jelly Roll (crossover)

### Emerging Sub-Trends
- **Alt-country/Americana crossover:** Tyler Childers, Sierra Ferrell, and Charley Crockett are selling out theaters and moving to amphitheaters — strong resale upside due to undersized venues vs. demand
- **Country-pop pipeline:** Megan Moroney and Ella Langley represent a new wave drawing younger (Gen Z) female audiences
- **Festival saturation risk:** The number of country-themed festivals continues to grow, potentially diluting per-event demand

### Best Markets/Cities
Nashville, Dallas/Fort Worth, Houston, Atlanta, Charlotte, Tampa, Denver, Phoenix, Indianapolis. The Sun Belt dominance is clear — Southern and Western markets consistently outperform.

### Over-Touring Risk: ⚠️ Moderate
The top tier (Wallen, Combs) is disciplined about limiting dates. Mid-tier acts risk over-saturation as the country touring calendar becomes extremely crowded in summer 2026.

## 2. Hip-Hop/Rap

### Demand Trend: 📉 Declining from Peak, but Top-Heavy

Hip-hop's live touring market has softened from its 2018-2023 commercial peak. Reddit discussions and industry chatter consistently note declining ticket sales for mid-tier rap tours. However, the genre remains **extremely top-heavy** — elite acts still command massive numbers. The **Drake & Kendrick Lamar "Grand National Tour"** in 2025 grossed **$256.4 million** on the North American leg alone, selling **1.1 million tickets** (the highest-grossing co-headlining run ever). Billboard chart changes and a general cultural shift have rap in a transitional period.

2026 touring highlights: **Cardi B** (first headlining tour behind *AM I THE DRAMA?*), **Gunna**, **Khalid**, **Mariah the Scientist**, **YFN Lucci**.

### Venue Sizes
- **Stadium (40,000-70,000):** Only Drake, Kendrick, Travis Scott tier
- **Arena (12,000-20,000):** Cardi B, Future, Lil Baby
- **Amphitheater (5,000-12,000):** Gunna, 21 Savage solo runs
- **Theater/Club (1,000-5,000):** Large swath of mid-tier rappers struggling to fill rooms

### Secondary Market Markups
- **Elite tier (Drake, Kendrick, Travis):** 2-5x face, especially for floor sections
- **Cardi B (debut tour hype):** Likely 1.5-3x given pent-up demand and cultural moment
- **Mid-tier rap:** Frequently trades **below face value** — significant risk for brokers
- **Festival appearances:** Hip-hop headliners at Rolling Loud, etc. still command premium pass prices

### Key Artists Driving Demand
Drake, Kendrick Lamar, Travis Scott, Cardi B, Future, Metro Boomin, 21 Savage. The gap between the haves and have-nots is wider in hip-hop than any other genre.

### Emerging Sub-Trends
- **Rap concert cancellation/postponement risk** remains high — more so than any other genre
- **Podcast-to-tour pipeline:** Acts like Druski, Kai Cenat (live events) blurring the lines between hip-hop culture and comedy/content creator touring
- **Nostalgia cycle:** 2000s/2010s rap nostalgia tours (Lil Wayne, Nelly, Ludacris) performing well in amphitheater settings
- **Chart decline ≠ cultural death:** Rap is declining on Billboard Hot 100 (partly due to 2026 chart rule changes) but still dominates streaming and cultural conversation

### Best Markets/Cities
Atlanta, Houston, New York, Los Angeles, Chicago, Miami, Detroit, Philadelphia, DMV (DC/Maryland/Virginia)

### Over-Touring Risk: ⚠️ Low for Top Tier, High for Mid-Tier
Elite acts are disciplined. The mid-tier is saturated — too many artists touring with insufficient demand, leading to cancellations and below-face resale. **Brokers should be very selective** in this genre.

## 3. Pop

### Demand Trend: 📈 Very Strong — The Dominant Genre for Resale

Pop remains the single most lucrative genre for ticket resale in 2026. The "Eras Tour effect" permanently reset fan expectations and willingness to pay for premium pop experiences. 2026 tours trend toward **higher demand and wider price bands**, with dynamic pricing creating complex resale environments.

### Venue Sizes
- **Stadium (40,000-80,000):** The biggest pop acts (Billie Eilish, Sabrina Carpenter, Chappell Roan scale-up)
- **Arena (12,000-20,000):** The bread-and-butter venue for pop tours
- **Theater (2,000-5,000):** Emerging pop acts, indie-pop crossovers

### Secondary Market Markups
- **Stadium pop headliners:** 2-5x face for premium/floor; GA can hit 3-4x
- **Arena pop:** 1.5-3x on average; higher for weekend/hometown shows
- **Emerging pop (first arena tours):** Often the highest ROI — demand spikes dramatically when an artist levels up venue size
- **Pop concert resale is highly sensitive to social media virality** — a single TikTok moment can spike prices overnight

### Key Artists Driving Demand
Sabrina Carpenter, Chappell Roan, Billie Eilish, Olivia Rodrigo, Dua Lipa, SZA (pop-adjacent), Tate McRae, Gracie Abrams, Reneé Rapp. The post-Eras Tour landscape has elevated a new generation of pop headliners.

### Emerging Sub-Trends
- **VIP/premium tier explosion:** High-end packages ($500-$2,000+) selling out as fast as GA at pop shows
- **Dynamic pricing vs. resale:** Ticketmaster's dynamic pricing is absorbing some resale margin, but demand still outstrips supply for the biggest acts
- **"Girlhood" touring boom:** Female pop acts (Carpenter, Roan, Rodrigo, Rapp) driving a generational wave of predominantly young female audiences willing to pay premium prices
- **Residencies gaining traction:** Following Adele's Vegas model, more pop acts considering residencies

### Best Markets/Cities
Los Angeles, New York, Chicago, Dallas, Toronto, London (for US acts crossing over). Pop performs well everywhere but premium pricing holds strongest in major metros.

### Over-Touring Risk: ⚠️ Low for Top Tier
Major pop acts are strategic about scarcity. The risk is more about **venue size miscalculation** — an artist who jumps to stadiums too fast can see resale prices crater.

## 4. Indie/Alternative

### Demand Trend: 📈 Growing — A Cornerstone Year

2026 is described as "a cornerstone year for indie and alternative scenes" with a rare convergence of milestone anniversaries, debut albums, and expansive international tours. Pitchfork's "66 Most Anticipated Tours of Spring 2026" is heavily weighted toward indie/alt acts. The genre benefits from passionate, dedicated fanbases willing to travel for shows.

### Venue Sizes
- **Arena (10,000-20,000):** Rare — only for breakthrough crossover acts
- **Theater/Large Club (2,000-5,000):** The sweet spot for indie headliners
- **Club (500-1,500):** Emerging acts, which is where resale opportunity is highest
- **Festival stages:** Significant presence at Lollapalooza, Bonnaroo, Pitchfork Fest, Treefort

### Secondary Market Markups
- **Hyped indie acts in small venues:** 2-4x face — the mismatch between venue size and demand creates the biggest resale premiums in music
- **Theater headliners:** 1.3-1.8x for sold-out shows
- **Festival passes with strong indie lineups:** 1.2-1.5x
- **Niche/cult acts:** Can command surprising premiums (3-5x) for one-off or reunion shows

### Key Artists Driving Demand
Ichiko Aoba (ambient folk sensation), The Last Dinner Party ("From The Pyre" tour), Ethel Cain, Fall Out Boy (emo/alt crossover), Fontaines D.C., Clairo, Turnstile, The New Pornographers, Guided by Voices, Beach House. Also: anniversary tours and reunion shows create unpredictable spikes.

### Emerging Sub-Trends
- **Emo/pop-punk revival:** Continued strong demand for acts like Fall Out Boy, My Chemical Romance alumni, and new-wave emo (bringing 2000s nostalgia audiences)
- **International indie breaking US:** Japanese (Ichiko Aoba), British (Last Dinner Party), Irish (Fontaines D.C.) acts increasingly selling out US theaters
- **Small-venue scarcity premium:** Indie's natural home is 500-2,000 cap venues, which creates automatic supply constraints and resale opportunity
- **Festival dependency:** Many indie acts are more valuable as festival draws than as standalone headliners

### Best Markets/Cities
Brooklyn/NYC, Los Angeles, Chicago, Portland, Seattle, Austin, Philadelphia, San Francisco, Denver, Nashville (increasingly)

### Over-Touring Risk: ⚠️ Low to Moderate
Most indie acts tour modestly. The risk is more about **genre fatigue** in oversaturated markets (Brooklyn, LA) where there are shows every night.

## 5. Latin (Regional Mexican, Reggaeton, Latin Pop, Latin Urban)

### Demand Trend: 📈📈 Surging — The Fastest-Growing Genre in Live Music

Latin music is the most explosive growth story in the 2026 touring market. Regional Mexican, reggaeton, Latin pop, and urbano are dominating charts, streaming platforms, and live stages globally. When Latin stars lead festival lineups, "demand explodes" (Hola). The convergence of urbano and regional Mexican sounds — visible at festivals like **Baja Beach Fest 2026** (headlined by Anuel AA, Junior H, Ozuna, Nicky Jam) — is creating a new mega-genre with massive touring potential.

### Venue Sizes
- **Stadium (40,000-70,000):** Karol G, Bad Bunny (when touring), Peso Pluma
- **Arena (12,000-20,000):** Fuerza Regida, Junior H, Anuel AA, Ozuna, Grupo Frontera
- **Amphitheater (5,000-12,000):** Los Tigres del Norte, Eden Muñoz, Xavi
- **Theater (2,000-5,000):** Emerging regional Mexican and Latin urban acts

### Secondary Market Markups
- **Stadium Latin headliners:** 2-4x face value; Karol G and Bad Bunny can hit 5x+
- **Arena tours:** 1.5-3x, especially for Fuerza Regida and regional Mexican acts selling to passionate, underserved markets
- **GA/floor for reggaeton:** Consistently 2-3x face
- **Latin festival passes:** 1.5-2.5x as festivals like Baja Beach Fest are perennial sellouts
- **General Latin concert tickets start $50-$100 face; premium/VIP $150-$400+** (SeatGeek)

### Key Artists Driving Demand
Karol G, Fuerza Regida, Junior H, Bad Bunny, Peso Pluma, Anuel AA, Ozuna, Grupo Frontera, Eden Muñoz, Xavi, Los Tigres del Norte, Ricardo Arjona, Carlos Santana. The depth of the Latin touring market is remarkable.

### Emerging Sub-Trends
- **Urbano-Regional Mexican convergence:** The biggest trend in all of live music. Acts like Junior H and Eden Muñoz alongside reggaeton mainstays on the same lineups signals a cultural merger
- **Latin music cruise tours:** Billboard editors predict Latin music cruises will explode in 2026
- **Reggae/dancehall fusion:** Latin artists increasingly incorporating Caribbean influences
- **U.S. Hispanic market growth:** Demographic tailwinds continue — the Latin live music audience is younger and growing faster than any other demographic
- **"Comeback" of early 2010s reggaeton sound:** Raw, street-heavy bangers returning to prominence

### Best Markets/Cities
Los Angeles, Houston, Dallas, Miami, Chicago, Phoenix, San Antonio, San Diego/Tijuana border region, New York, Las Vegas. Markets with large Hispanic populations are obvious, but Latin shows are increasingly selling well in non-traditional markets.

### Over-Touring Risk: ⚠️ Low
Demand continues to outstrip supply. The genre is still in an expansion phase where most acts could add dates and sell them. Some festival saturation risk as new Latin-focused festivals launch.

## 6. Electronic/Dance (House, Techno, Bass, Festival Circuit)

### Demand Trend: 📈 Strong and Expanding — Most-Booked Genre at US Festivals

Electronic music has "cemented its popularity in the mainstream, emerging as the **most-booked genre at US festivals**" in 2025-2026 (Booking Agent Info via EDM Identity). Gen Z is driving growth, with festivals like EDC (400K+ attendance), Ultra Miami, and international events (Awakenings, Sónar) continuing to expand.

### Venue Sizes
- **Mega-festival (50,000-400,000):** EDC Las Vegas, Ultra Miami, Tomorrowlands
- **Festival (10,000-50,000):** Lightning in a Bottle, Project GLOW DC, Creamfields, Parklife
- **Club/Warehouse (500-5,000):** The core of the scene — weekly events in every major city
- **Arena (one-offs) (10,000-20,000):** Skrillex, deadmau5, specific event productions

### Secondary Market Markups
- **Major festival passes (EDC, Ultra):** 1.3-2x face, with VIP/luxury packages selling out first and commanding 2-3x
- **Club shows by top DJs:** Limited resale market; many shows are priced to clear at the door
- **Arena one-offs (Skrillex, special events):** 1.5-2.5x
- **Boutique festivals (LiB, Dirtybird):** 1.5-2x; these tend to sell out early and have strong resale demand

### Key Artists Driving Demand
Fred Again.., Skrillex, Fisher, John Summit, Peggy Gou, Nina Kraviz, Four Tet, Ben UFO, Subtronics, Excision (bass), Charlotte de Witte, Amelie Lens. The DJ/producer model means individual show demand is more fragmented than in other genres.

### Emerging Sub-Trends
- **Higher BPMs and hardgroove:** Techno trending faster and harder in 2026, with hardware-only live performances gaining prestige
- **Luxury festival packages:** High-end bundles selling out as fast as GA — travel agencies offering full luxury festival experiences
- **Sustainability focus:** Eco-friendly initiatives becoming a selling point for festivals
- **Club culture renaissance:** Intimate, underground-focused events growing in popularity as a counter to mega-festival culture
- **Cross-genre bookings:** Electronic acts increasingly appearing on non-EDM festival lineups

### Best Markets/Cities
Las Vegas (EDC), Miami (Ultra/WMC), Los Angeles, Chicago, Denver, Detroit (techno roots), Brooklyn, DC (Project GLOW), Ibiza, Berlin, Amsterdam, Barcelona (Sónar)

### Over-Touring Risk: ⚠️ Moderate for Festivals
The number of EDM festivals continues to grow, potentially diluting per-event attendance. However, club-level shows remain robust. DJ burnout is a real factor — top DJs play 150-200+ shows per year.

## 7. R&B/Soul

### Demand Trend: 📈 Growing — Nostalgia + New Wave

R&B is experiencing a dual-wave resurgence: legacy/nostalgia tours from 90s-2000s icons are selling briskly, while a new generation of alternative R&B artists is building touring muscle. The genre benefits from strong cross-demographic appeal and performs exceptionally well as live entertainment.

### Venue Sizes
- **Arena (12,000-20,000):** Usher, New Edition, legacy package tours
- **Amphitheater (5,000-12,000):** Keith Sweat, B2K, SZA (when touring R&B venues)
- **Theater (1,500-5,000):** Khalid, Mariah the Scientist, emerging R&B
- **Club (500-1,500):** Alternative R&B acts

### Secondary Market Markups
- **Legacy tours (New Edition, Usher):** 1.5-2.5x face; driven by nostalgia demand from millennials and Gen X with disposable income
- **"Queens of Soul" package tours:** Rapid sellouts, 2x+ resale premiums
- **New-gen R&B (SZA solo, Khalid):** 1.3-2x
- **R&B ONLY LIVE (party/experience events):** Growing format with 1.5-2x resale on sold-out dates
- **Mid-tier R&B:** Can trade below face — genre has a soft middle

### Key Artists Driving Demand
Usher, SZA (R&B-adjacent), New Edition, Keith Sweat, B2K, Khalid, Mariah the Scientist, Summer Walker, Daniel Caesar, Brent Faiyaz, Gabriel Iglesias (comedy crossover with R&B audiences). Legacy package tours (multiple classic R&B acts on one bill) are a major revenue driver.

### Emerging Sub-Trends
- **R&B nostalgia economy:** 90s and 2000s R&B package tours are a reliable resale play — think Jodeci, SWV, Keith Sweat, Boyz II Men combinations
- **Alternative R&B live growth:** Artists like Steve Lacy, Daniel Caesar, and Brent Faiyaz moving from clubs to theaters
- **"Experience" R&B events:** R&B ONLY LIVE, Lovers & Friends Festival, and similar curated experiences blending party culture with live performance
- **Soul music legends touring:** Historic multi-artist packages selling out and adding dates due to high demand

### Best Markets/Cities
Atlanta, New York, Houston, Chicago, Los Angeles, DMV, Detroit, Memphis, New Orleans, Philadelphia. R&B has its strongest markets in cities with large Black populations and strong musical heritage.

### Over-Touring Risk: ⚠️ Low to Moderate
Legacy acts are limited by age/health — scarcity is built in. New-gen R&B acts are not over-touring. The nostalgia lane has runway for several more years.

## 8. Rock/Metal (Classic Rock Reunions, Metalcore, Punk Revival)

### Demand Trend: 📈 Stable to Growing — Reunion Driven + Genre Revival

Rock/metal in 2026 is powered by two forces: **legacy reunion/farewell tours** commanding enormous secondary premiums, and a **genuine genre revival** led by metalcore, punk, and post-hardcore acts building fervent young fanbases.

### Venue Sizes
- **Stadium (40,000-70,000):** Iron Maiden, Linkin Park (reunion), System of a Down (reunion dates in Mexico City)
- **Arena (10,000-20,000):** Electric Callboy, Bad Omens with Beartooth, Limp Bizkit
- **Festival (10,000-80,000):** Resurrection Fest (Iron Maiden, Limp Bizkit, Marilyn Manson), Rock am Ring, Download
- **Theater/Club (1,000-5,000):** Metalcore (Thornhill, Erra, Currents), punk (PUP, Face to Face)
- **Niche festival (5,000-15,000):** Camp Punksylvania (Body Count, PUP, Face to Face), Grim Reefer Fest

### Secondary Market Markups
- **Reunion/farewell tours:** 2-5x face — these are one-time events and command huge premiums. System of a Down reunion dates will be among the highest-markup shows of 2026
- **Major festival headliners (Iron Maiden):** 1.5-2.5x for festival passes
- **Metalcore arena tours (Electric Callboy, Bad Omens):** 1.3-2x; this genre is in a growth phase
- **Punk/hardcore club shows:** Low resale volume but can spike to 2-3x for sellouts
- **Classic rock amphitheater tours:** 1.2-1.5x; older demographic is price-sensitive

### Key Artists Driving Demand
Iron Maiden, Linkin Park (reunion), System of a Down (reunion), Electric Callboy, Bad Omens, Beartooth, Erra, Currents, Thornhill, PUP, Knocked Loose, Sleep Token, Turnstile (crossover), Fall Out Boy, Good Kid

### Emerging Sub-Trends
- **Metalcore mainstream breakthrough:** Electric Callboy and Bad Omens are filling arenas — the genre is moving from clubs to major venues
- **Punk revival (Camp Punksylvania, etc.):** Dedicated punk festivals thriving with multi-generational appeal
- **Reunion economics:** Every System of a Down or Rage Against the Machine reunion is an automatic resale goldmine due to extreme scarcity
- **J-rock/anime-adjacent rock:** Good Kid and similar acts drawing from anime/gaming culture audiences
- **European festival circuit remains dominant** for rock/metal, with Resurrection Fest, Rock am Ring, and Download as tentpoles

### Best Markets/Cities
London, Berlin, São Paulo, Mexico City, Los Angeles, New York, Chicago, Detroit, Minneapolis, Madrid/Barcelona. Rock/metal has a more international profile than most genres.

### Over-Touring Risk: ⚠️ Low for Reunions, Moderate for Active Bands
Reunion tours are inherently scarce. Active metalcore bands face some risk as the number of tours grows, but the genre's fanbase is passionate and willing to attend multiple shows per year.

## 9. K-Pop

### Demand Trend: 📈📈📈 Explosive — The Highest-Margin Genre for Resale

K-pop is the single highest-margin genre in the secondary ticket market in 2026, driven primarily by **BTS's Arirang Tour** — the group's first tour since members completed military service. BTS tickets on StubHub reached **$7,276 — nearly 40x the original face value** (CNBC, Feb 2026). The BTS tour is triggering a **concert tourism boom**, with hotel searches jumping **6,700%** in tour cities (CNBC). TWICE, IVE, and other groups add additional depth to the market.

### Venue Sizes
- **Stadium (40,000-80,000):** BTS (multiple nights per city)
- **Arena (15,000-20,000):** TWICE, BLACKPINK members (solo), Stray Kids, IVE
- **Theater (3,000-8,000):** Smaller groups, solo artists (Baekhyun, Taemin — Taemin's Vegas show "sold out in 10 minutes")

### Secondary Market Markups
- **BTS:** 10-40x face value — the most extreme markups in all of live entertainment. Floor/close seats routinely $3,000-$7,000+
- **TWICE/Stray Kids/IVE:** 3-4x face value typical for good seats
- **Solo K-pop artists (Baekhyun, Taemin):** 2-5x, especially for limited US dates
- **K-pop is THE resale opportunity of 2026** — nothing else comes close on margin

### Key Artists Driving Demand
BTS (Arirang Tour — the biggest tour in the world in 2026), TWICE, IVE, Stray Kids, Baekhyun, Taemin, ENHYPEN, LE SSERAFIM, (G)I-DLE, aespa

### Emerging Sub-Trends
- **Concert tourism:** K-pop fans traveling internationally to follow tours — fans like the one profiled by The Guardian planning to attend 11 cities/22 shows, spending $6,000+. This is not unusual.
- **Verified fan programs:** Ticketmaster's verified fan for K-pop is intensifying, making initial ticket acquisition harder and driving resale higher
- **Bot/reseller crackdown vs. demand:** Professional resellers using automated purchasing systems remain a factor despite platform efforts
- **"K-pop bubble" concerns:** Some industry watchers question whether the US K-pop concert market could be overextended for acts below the BTS/BLACKPINK tier — not all K-pop acts sell well live in the US
- **4th-gen groups scaling up:** IVE, LE SSERAFIM, and others moving from theaters to arenas

### Best Markets/Cities
Los Angeles, New York, Chicago, Dallas, Las Vegas, Seattle, Toronto, San Francisco. K-pop performs strongest in cities with large Asian-American populations and major airports (for international fan travel).

### Over-Touring Risk: ⚠️ Low for Top Tier, High for Lower Tier
BTS, TWICE, and top groups are supply-constrained (limited US dates). The risk is in the **long tail** of K-pop acts that book US arenas and can't fill them — brokers should stick to the proven headliners.

## 10. Comedy (Standup, Podcast Tours)

### Demand Trend: 📈 Bright Outlook After 2025 Softening

Comedy touring surged post-pandemic but hit a "rough patch in 2025" according to CAA's Matt Blake, partly due to Ticketmaster's all-in pricing rollout and a government shutdown softening the market. However, the **forecast for 2026 is "extremely bright"** (Pollstar Comedy Executive Survey). The genre continues its arena-ification trend, with more comedians emulating major music acts by playing multiple arena shows in key markets.

### Venue Sizes
- **Arena (10,000-20,000):** Sebastian Maniscalco, Bert Kreischer, Gabriel Iglesias, Matt Rife, Nate Bargatze
- **Theater (2,000-5,000):** The primary venue for most working comedians — Taylor Tomlinson, Leanne Morgan, Fortune Feimster, Jeff Arcuri
- **Club (200-800):** Development/emerging acts, podcast tapings
- **Non-traditional (3,000-6,000+):** Jeff Arcuri selling 3,000-6,000 per market as a relatively new act

### Secondary Market Markups
- **Arena comedians (Maniscalco, Kreischer):** 1.5-2.5x face; comedy audiences are generally more price-sensitive than music fans
- **"Moment" comedians (Matt Rife, viral acts):** 2-3x when demand spikes from social media
- **Podcast tours:** 1.2-1.5x; depends heavily on the specific podcast's fanbase
- **Michael McIntyre:** Record-breaking 28 shows at London's O2, 1.5 million total tickets — UK comedy is its own market
- **Below-face risk:** Comedy has more shows trading below face than music — many comedians add dates until demand is met

### Key Artists Driving Demand
Sebastian Maniscalco, Bert Kreischer, Gabriel Iglesias, Matt Rife, Nate Bargatze, Taylor Tomlinson, Leanne Morgan, Trevor Noah, Jeff Dunham, Andrew Schulz, Katt Williams, Shane Gillis, Jeff Arcuri, Russell Howard (UK), Jimmy Carr

### Emerging Sub-Trends
- **Podcast-to-tour pipeline:** "Two Dykes and a Mic" sold 10,000+ tickets; podcast audiences convert reliably to live show attendees
- **Decentralization from NYC/LA:** Joe Rogan built an empire in Austin; Nate Bargatze runs from Nashville. Comics no longer need coastal bases
- **"Canceled" comedians as a sub-market:** Controversial comics draw passionate audiences willing to pay premiums
- **Phone-free shows:** Russell Howard's 2026 tour is explicitly "no phones, no distractions" — a growing trend
- **English-language comedians succeeding in non-English markets:** International comedy touring is expanding

### Best Markets/Cities
New York, Los Angeles, Chicago, Nashville (growing), Austin, Dallas, Atlanta, Denver, Boston, London, Dublin

### Over-Touring Risk: ⚠️ Moderate to High
Comedy's biggest risk is **date saturation**. Unlike music artists who play one city per tour, comedians routinely add 3-5 shows per market until demand is absorbed. This structurally limits resale premiums. Brokers should focus on **initial on-sale weekends** before additional dates are announced.

## 11. Jam Band / Festival Circuit

### Demand Trend: 📈 Strong — Phish at the Sphere is the Story of 2026

The jam band world in 2026 revolves around one event: **Phish at the Sphere in Las Vegas** (April 16-20, 2026 — sold out). Fresh off that residency, Phish has announced a **21-date summer tour** including 5 nights at MSG and a return to Fenway Park. The tour is averaging **$991 per ticket across 14 shows on secondary markets** (Gametime), making it one of the highest-grossing jam-band tours in history. **Goose** is the breakout act, having sold out MSG for the first time and released two acclaimed albums in 2025.

### Venue Sizes
- **Sphere (17,500):** Phish — a first for the jam band genre
- **Arena (15,000-20,000):** Phish (MSG runs), Dead & Company (Sphere residency in 2024 set the template)
- **Amphitheater/Shed (5,000-15,000):** Goose, Billy Strings, Widespread Panic
- **Festival (10,000-30,000):** Bonnaroo, Summer Camp, Peach Fest, Lockn'

## 12. Classical/Symphony/Gaming Symphonies

### Demand Trend: 📈 Niche but Growing — Gaming Symphonies Are the Breakout

Traditional classical/symphony attendance remains stable but flat for standard repertoire. The **gaming symphony** sub-genre, however, is a genuine growth story. Video game concert formats are proliferating: **Video Games Live**, **Game Music Festival** (London's Fairfield Halls and Royal Festival Hall), **Final Symphony** (Final Fantasy world tour), **Game ON!**, and individual productions like **The Music of Square Enix** at the Chicago Symphony Orchestra and **Baldur's Gate III world premiere** at NFM Philharmonic. These events attract younger, passionate audiences who rarely attend traditional classical performances.

### Venue Sizes
- **Symphony hall (1,500-3,000):** Standard classical and gaming symphony performances
- **Arena/large venue (5,000-15,000):** Major gaming symphony tours (Video Games Live)
- **Festival hall (1,000-2,500):** Game Music Festival format
- **University/community (500-1,500):** Gamer Symphony Orchestra (University of Maryland's 20th anniversary in 2026)

### Secondary Market Markups
- **Gaming symphonies (Zelda, Final Fantasy, Hades, Baldur's Gate):** 1.5-2.5x face — these consistently sell out and attract audiences who are not price-sensitive
- **Traditional classical:** Rarely trades above face on secondary market; most inventory available at or below face
- **Special event classical (major soloists, rare programs):** 1.2-1.5x for prestige events
- **Holiday programs (Nutcracker, etc.):** 1.3-1.8x for premium dates

### Key Properties Driving Demand
Video Games Live (touring), Final Symphony (Final Fantasy), Game Music Festival (Diablo "Infernal Symphony," Hades "Ballads of the Underworld"), The Music of Square Enix (CSO), Game ON! (touring), Baldur's Gate III world premiere concert. On the traditional side: major soloists and conductor-driven events.

### Emerging Sub-Trends
- **Gaming-to-concert pipeline:** The audience for gaming symphonies is the 18-35 demographic that classical music has been desperate to attract. These events serve as gateway experiences.
- **IP-specific concerts:** Individual game franchises (Zelda, Final Fantasy, Hades, Diablo, Baldur's Gate) are each spawning dedicated concert experiences
- **Franchise depth:** Square Enix alone supports multiple touring productions
- **Co-branding with gaming companies:** Larian Studios (Baldur's Gate), Supergiant Games (Hades) actively partnering with orchestras
- **Regional symphony survival strategy:** Many regional orchestras are programming gaming concerts as revenue diversification

### Best Markets/Cities
London, New York, Chicago, Los Angeles, San Francisco, Tokyo, Berlin, Wrocław (Game Music Festival home), major university towns

### Over-Touring Risk: ⚠️ Low
Gaming symphony is still early in its growth curve. Traditional classical has natural scarcity (orchestras have fixed seasons). The risk is in **IP fatigue** — too many game franchises launching concerts simultaneously.
