## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY — TOP 5 OPPORTUNITIES

### 1. 🔥 Dodgers Home Games at Camelback Ranch
- **Why:** Back-to-back World Series champs. Added Kyle Tucker ($240M). Ohtani + Betts + Tucker = most stacked spring roster ever.
- **Face:** $45 (lawn) – $100 (home plate)
- **Resale:** $50–$200+ for hot games; Mexico WBC exhibition (Mar 4) commands $100+ get-in
- **Play:** Buy any Dodgers home game face value → flip for 2–3x on weekends. The Dodgers vs. Cubs split-squad (Feb 28) and Dodgers vs. Giants (Mar 18) are premium matchups.

### 2. 🔥 Cubs at Sloan Park (Bregman's Debut)
- **Why:** Alex Bregman signed 5yr/$175M with Cubs. Largest capacity in Cactus League (15,000) but they sell out most games anyway. Reddit confirms: "at this point the Cubs sell out most games."
- **Face:** $24.50 (berm) – $68 (infield box)
- **Resale:** $40–$141 for premium matchups
- **Play:** Cubs vs. Yankees (Mar 23–24) is HISTORIC — first Yankees visit to AZ spring training since 1951 (Mickey Mantle era). These will be the hottest tickets of the entire spring. Buy immediately.

### 3. 🔥 Yankees at Steinbrenner Field (Grapefruit League)
- **Why:** Perennial sellout machine in Florida. Added Cody Bellinger. Spencer Jones (top prospect) making waves.
- **Face:** ~$30–$75
- **Resale:** Yankees games sell out at EVERY visiting venue too. "Yankees games sell out here in Dunedin" (Reddit). Yankees at Red Sox (Mar 4) is peak demand.
- **Play:** Any Yankees home game, especially weekends. Yankees visiting smaller venues (TD Ballpark/Dunedin) = guaranteed sellout = resale premium.

### 4. 🔥 WBC Exhibition Games (Mar 3–4)
- **Why:** One-time-only events. Team USA vs. Giants at Scottsdale Stadium (Mar 3). Team Mexico vs. Dodgers at Camelback (Mar 4). Yankees vs. Red Sox (Mar 4). These are unique collectible experiences.
- **Resale:** WBC exhibitions at spring training venues are novelty events with built-in scarcity. Expect 2–4x face value.
- **Play:** Buy every WBC exhibition ticket you can at face value. These will NOT be available day-of.

### 5. 🔥 Weekend Games at High-Demand Venues (Any Team)
- **Why:** Saturday/Sunday games consistently outsell weekday by 40–60%. Late March weekends (spring break season + rosters solidifying) are peak.
- **Play:** Target Sat/Sun games at Camelback (Dodgers), Sloan Park (Cubs), Scottsdale Stadium (Giants), and Steinbrenner Field (Yankees). Buy face → resale markup.

## KEY TAKEAWAYS FOR ZACH

1. **Dodgers are the #1 money-maker** — every home game has resale potential. The Kyle Tucker addition makes them even more dominant. Face value lawn tickets at $45 are the best buy-to-flip in spring training.

2. **Cubs vs. Yankees (Mar 23–24) is THE game of spring** — historic matchup, two massive fanbases, Bregman vs. Judge. If you can get face value tickets, expect 3–4x return.

3. **WBC exhibitions are one-time events** — Team USA at Scottsdale, Mexico at Camelback. These only happen every 3 years. Buy and hold until game day for maximum premium.

4. **Avoid low-demand midweek games** at Goodyear, Surprise, Charlotte Sports Park (Rays), LECOM Park (Pirates) — these won't flip. Resale is at or below face.

5. **White Sox games are a sleeper** at Camelback Ranch — same venue as Dodgers, tickets are HALF the price ($24.50 lawn), and Konnor Griffin (#1 prospect) is drawing attention. If Griffin goes viral mid-spring, CWS tickets could spike.

6. **Florida is harder to broker** — venues are spread across the state, harder logistics. Focus on Steinbrenner Field (Yankees) and JetBlue Park (Red Sox) for Florida flips.

7. **Parking is NOT worth the effort** — most high-demand Cactus League venues have free parking. Florida venues have paid parking but thin margins.
