## Key Patterns & Anomalies

### 1. The "Graduation Pattern" (Most Valuable Signal for Blackbeard)
Artists selling out sub-2K venues are about to graduate to bigger venues. The window to profit is **6-12 months** before graduation. Key indicators:
- Spotify ML crosses 1M → they'll sell out 1,000-cap venues within 3-6 months
- Adding second shows in same city → demand exceeding supply
- Venue upgrades announced (moved from 1,200 to 2,500 cap) → signal to buy remaining small dates

**2025 Examples:**
- Chappell Roan: Moved from The Truman (KC, 1,200 cap) to The Midland (3,500 cap) mid-tour in 2024
- Ethel Cain: From 1,500-cap theaters to Radio City Music Hall & Shrine Auditorium
- MK.gee: Oversold venues in 2025, indicating imminent venue upgrades

### 2. TikTok Virality ≠ Ticket Sales (The Conversion Gap)
Having viral TikTok songs does NOT reliably predict sellouts unless accompanied by:
- Spotify ML above genre thresholds
- Instagram engagement (not just followers)
- Prior touring history

**Red flag:** Artists with 500K+ TikTok followers but <100K Spotify ML often can't sell 200-cap rooms. TikTok followers have ~5% ticket conversion vs Instagram's ~15%.

### 3. Genre Multipliers

| Genre | Sellout Multiplier vs Base | Why |
|-------|---------------------------|-----|
| Hardcore/Metal | 1.5x | Higher per-capita ticket buying; scene loyalty |
| Indie/Alt | 1.0x (baseline) | Standard conversion |
| Pop | 0.8x | Wider but shallower fanbase; more casual listeners |
| Comedy | 1.3x | Comedians sell more tickets per follower than musicians |
| Electronic/DJ | 0.7x | Festival-oriented; club show conversion lower |
| Country (emerging) | 1.4x | Strong regional loyalty; dedicated fanbase |

### 4. The "3M Spotify / 500K IG" Sweet Spot
For sub-2K venue prediction, the most reliable combination is:
- **Spotify ML: 1M-3M**
- **Instagram: 200K-800K**
- **Active touring history (not first tour)**
- **New album within 6 months**

This combination predicted sellouts for: Ethel Cain, MK.gee, Amyl and the Sniffers, and Knocked Loose in 2025.

## Actionable Rules for Blackbeard

### Rule 1: "The 2M Rule" (Primary Trigger)
**IF** artist Spotify ML crosses 2M **AND** they announce a tour at venues ≤2,000 cap **AND** they have a new release within 6 months **THEN** predict sellout with 85% confidence.

### Rule 2: "The Comedy 500K Rule"
**IF** comedian has 500K+ combined IG+TikTok **AND** they have a Netflix/YouTube special **AND** theater tour announced (800-2,000 cap) **THEN** predict sellout with 80% confidence.

### Rule 3: "The Graduation Signal" (Highest Value)
**IF** artist adds second shows in multiple cities **OR** venue upgrades announced **THEN** remaining original dates will sell out. Buy immediately.

### Rule 4: "The Genre Adjustment"
**FOR** hardcore/metal/punk artists: Lower the Spotify threshold by 40%. An artist with 800K Spotify ML in hardcore will sell out the same 1,500-cap room that requires 1.5M for an indie artist.

### Rule 5: "The TikTok Discount"
**IF** artist's TikTok followers > 5x their Spotify ML **THEN** reduce sellout confidence by 20%. The audience is passive/viral, not ticket-buying.

### Rule 6: "Album Cycle Timing"
**IF** tour dates fall within 2-6 months of album release **THEN** increase sellout confidence by 15%. The hype window is real.

### New Rule Additions

**Rule 7: "The Compression Signal" (Highest Alpha)**
**IF** artist books venues at ≤50% of their expected Spotify ML capacity band **THEN** instant sellout guaranteed, secondary market premium 3-5x. Priority buy.

**Rule 8: "The Comedy TikTok Correction"**
**FOR** comedians: TikTok followers count at full value (not discounted like music). A comedian with 2M TikTok followers is comparable to one with 2M Instagram followers for sellout prediction.

**Rule 9: "The Prestige/Grammy Multiplier"**
**IF** artist wins major award (Grammy, Emmy) **AND** has <1M Spotify ML **THEN** apply 2x multiplier to expected capacity. Award visibility creates ticket demand disproportionate to streaming numbers. (Samara Joy evidence.)

**Rule 10: "The Volume vs. Scarcity Model"**
Artists follow one of two tour strategies:
- **Scarcity model** (Geese, Bar Italia): Few dates, instant sellouts, high secondary premiums → Best for resale profit
- **Volume model** (Atsuko, Mark Normand): Many dates, steady sellouts, lower secondary premiums → Better for primary market analytics
